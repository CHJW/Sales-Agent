## 1. SCORING/MARKING CRITERIA

### Three-Stage Evaluation Process

**Stage 1: Compliance Assessment (Pass/Fail)**
- Verification of all mandatory criteria
- Document completeness and format compliance  
- Basic capability and experience validation
- Financial and insurance verification

**Stage 2: Technical Evaluation (Weighted Scoring)**

| Evaluation Criteria | Weighting | Maximum Points | Focus Areas |
|---------------------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| **SME & Aboriginal Participation** | 25% | 250 points | Local resources, Indigenous engagement, social procurement |
| **Service Delivery Methodology** | 25% | 250 points | ITIL processes, service management, quality assurance |
| **Track Record & Experience** | 20% | 200 points | Government experience, Salesforce implementations, references |
| **Capability & Capacity** | 20% | 200 points | Team qualifications, certifications, resource availability |
| **Value for Money** | 10% | 100 points | Total cost of ownership, pricing competitiveness |
| **Total** | **100%** | **1000 points** | |

**Stage 3: Due Diligence and Final Assessment**
- Reference checks and client interviews
- Financial due diligence and verification
- Security clearance and background checks
- Final commercial negotiation and contract finalization

### Scoring Methodology
Each criterion is scored on a scale of 0-5 where:
- 5 = Excellent (exceptional capability, exceeds requirements significantly)
- 4 = Very Good (strong capability, exceeds requirements)  
- 3 = Good (adequate capability, meets requirements)
- 2 = Satisfactory (minimal capability, barely meets requirements)
- 1 = Poor (inadequate capability, does not meet requirements)
- 0 = Unacceptable (no capability, fails to address requirements)

Raw scores are multiplied by weighting percentage to determine final points.

### Detailed Evaluation Criteria Breakdown

#### 1. SME & Aboriginal Participation (25% - 250 points)

**Sub-criteria and Weighting:**
- **Local Resource Utilization (40%):** Geographic distribution of delivery team
- **SME Participation (30%):** Engagement of NSW small and medium enterprises
- **Aboriginal Business Participation (20%):** Indigenous-owned business involvement
- **Social Procurement (10%):** Community benefit initiatives and outcomes

**Assessment Focus:**
- Percentage of contract value committed to SME participation (minimum 20% expected)
- Number and value of Aboriginal business opportunities identified
- Quality of proposed SME/Aboriginal supplier identification process
- Mentoring and development programs offered
- Payment terms and support for small businesses
- Monitoring, reporting, and accountability measures

#### 2. Service Delivery Methodology (25% - 250 points)

**Sub-criteria and Weighting:**
- **Service Management Framework (30%):** ITIL alignment and process maturity
- **Quality Assurance (25%):** Testing, change management, and quality controls
- **Innovation and Continuous Improvement (20%):** Proactive improvement methodology
- **Integration with DCJ Operations (15%):** Collaboration model and governance
- **Risk Management (10%):** Comprehensive risk identification and mitigation

**Assessment Focus:**
- Alignment with ITIL service management best practices
- Integration with DCJ's existing processes and tools
- Quality assurance and service level management approach
- Change management and release management processes
- Communication and stakeholder engagement strategies
- Problem and incident management capabilities

#### 3. Track Record & Experience (20% - 200 points)

**Sub-criteria and Weighting:**
- **Government Sector Experience (35%):** Public sector implementations and outcomes
- **Salesforce Platform Expertise (30%):** Service Cloud and Experience Cloud implementations
- **Scale and Complexity (20%):** Large-scale implementations with similar user bases
- **Innovation and Excellence (15%):** Recognized achievements and awards

**Assessment Focus:**
- Number and value of similar Salesforce AMS contracts delivered
- Duration and success of ongoing managed services contracts
- Experience with Service Cloud, Experience Cloud, and MuleSoft platforms
- NSW Government or Australian Government project experience
- Understanding of human services sector requirements and constraints

#### 4. Capability & Capacity (20% - 200 points)

**Sub-criteria and Weighting:**
- **Team Qualifications and Certifications (40%):** Salesforce certifications and relevant experience
- **Resource Availability and Scalability (25%):** Bench strength and surge capacity
- **Key Personnel Stability (20%):** Retention strategies and succession planning
- **Training and Development (15%):** Continuous learning and capability building

**Assessment Focus:**
- Number and qualifications of Salesforce certified personnel
- Availability of specialized skills (MuleSoft, Tableau CRM, etc.)
- Resource allocation and management approach
- Training and development programs
- Innovation capabilities and R&D investment

#### 5. Value for Money (10% - 100 points)

**Sub-criteria and Weighting:**
- **Total Cost of Ownership (50%):** 7-year TCO including all costs and considerations
- **Pricing Competitiveness (25%):** Market rate comparison and value justification
- **Cost Optimization (15%):** Efficiency gains and cost reduction strategies
- **Commercial Terms (10%):** Payment terms, risk allocation, and contract flexibility

**Assessment Focus:**
- Competitiveness of hourly rates and monthly managed service fees
- Total 3-year contract value compared to market benchmarks
- Additional services included at no cost
- Cost optimization opportunities and efficiency improvements

## 2. MANDATORY/PASS-FAIL REQUIREMENTS

### Mandatory Requirement #1 - Salesforce Partner Status
**Requirement:** Current Salesforce Partner (minimum Silver tier)
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 2, Section 3.1
**Evidence Required:**
- Current Salesforce Partner certificate
- Specialization certificates (Service Cloud, Experience Cloud preferred)
- Partner portal screenshot showing current status
- Account manager reference letter

### Mandatory Requirement #2 - Australian Presence and Local Delivery Capability  
**Requirement:** 
- Primary operations based in Australia
- Local delivery team based in Australia
- 24/7 support capability from Australian time zones
- Minimum 85% of delivery team based in Australia
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 2, Section 3.2

### Mandatory Requirement #3 - Security Certification and Framework
**Requirement:**
- ISO 27001:2013/2022 (Information Security Management Systems) OR
- SOC 2 Type II (Service Organization Control 2) OR  
- Equivalent security certification
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 2, Section 3.3
**Evidence Required:**
- Current security certification certificates
- ISMS policy summary document
- Security organization chart
- Recent security assessment or audit report

### Mandatory Requirement #4 - Security Clearance Capability
**Requirement:**
- Ability to obtain Baseline clearances for all required staff
- Ability to obtain Negative Vetting 1 (NV1) clearances for key personnel
- Demonstrated capability to meet character and suitability requirements
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 2, Section 3.4

### Mandatory Requirement #5 - Financial Capacity
**Requirement:**
- Minimum annual revenue of $100M in previous financial year
- Current liquidity ratio of 1.2:1 or better
- Audited financial statements for 3 most recent years
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 1, Section 2.5.2

### Mandatory Requirement #6 - Insurance Coverage
**Requirements:**
- Public Liability Insurance: $20M minimum coverage
- Professional Indemnity Insurance: $20M minimum coverage  
- Cyber Liability Insurance: $20M minimum coverage
- Workers Compensation: As required by NSW law
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 1, Section 2.5.1

### Mandatory Requirement #7 - Technical Capability
**Requirements:**
- Minimum 25 Salesforce certified professionals on delivery team
- Demonstrated experience with government sector implementations
- MuleSoft integration platform expertise
- Cybersecurity certifications and expertise
**Section Reference:** RFP Part 1, Section 2.6

### Failure to meet any mandatory criteria will result in proposal disqualification.

- Core Technical Requirements: Fundamental Salesforce platform requirements
- Mandatory Criteria: Qualification requirements and compliance standards

- Pricing Structure: Fundamental pricing model and rate structures

## STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

### Where is the weight concentrated?

**Technical vs Price vs Experience:**
- **Service Delivery & Technical Capability:** 45% (Service Delivery 25% + Capability 20%)
- **Experience & Track Record:** 20% 
- **Social Procurement:** 25% (SME & Aboriginal Participation)
- **Price/Value:** 10%

This weighting reveals that **technical capability and service delivery methodology are the primary focus**, with social procurement carrying equal weight to technical delivery methodology. Price is notably de-emphasized at only 10%.

### Unusual weightings or criteria

1. **Social Procurement Emphasis (25%):** Unusually high weighting for SME & Aboriginal participation, equal to service delivery methodology. This reflects NSW Government's strong commitment to social procurement.

2. **Low Price Weighting (10%):** Significantly lower than typical government procurements (usually 20-30%), indicating DCJ prioritizes quality and capability over cost.

3. **Aboriginal Business Participation:** Specific sub-criterion with 20% weighting within social procurement, demonstrating commitment to Indigenous economic participation.

4. **Local Delivery Emphasis:** Strong focus on Australian presence and local delivery capability as mandatory requirement, not just preference.

### What does this reveal about client priorities?

**Primary Client Priorities:**
1. **Service Excellence Over Cost:** Low price weighting indicates DCJ values quality delivery over lowest cost
2. **Social Responsibility:** High weighting for social procurement reflects NSW Government policy priorities
3. **Risk Mitigation:** Emphasis on security, compliance, and Australian presence shows risk-averse approach
4. **Long-term Partnership:** Focus on capability and methodology suggests desire for sustainable partnership
5. **Government Context:** Understanding of public sector requirements is critical to success

**Secondary Priorities:**
- Innovation and continuous improvement
- Knowledge transfer to internal teams
- Compliance with government standards
- Transparency and accountability

### Red flags or areas needing clarification

**Potential Risk Areas:**
1. **Social Procurement Targets:** 20% minimum SME participation may be challenging to achieve cost-effectively
2. **Security Clearance Timelines:** 6-8 week clearance processing could impact project timelines
3. **Dual Evaluation Panels:** Technical and business panel scoring could create conflicting priorities
4. **Mandatory vs Scored Criteria:** Some mandatory requirements also appear in scored criteria
5. **Short Negotiation Window:** 15 business days for commercial negotiation may be insufficient for complex terms

**Areas Requiring Clarification:**
1. Definition of "SME" for participation calculations
2. Specific Aboriginal business certification requirements
3. Measurement methodology for social procurement outcomes
4. Integration requirements with existing DCJ internal teams
5. Performance measurement frameworks for ongoing service delivery

**Contract Value vs Evaluation Emphasis:**
The significant contract value ($8.5M-$16M annually) warrants the comprehensive evaluation approach, but the complexity of evaluation criteria may create challenges in objective assessment and comparison of proposals.
