# Competitor Analysis: Deloitte

**RFP Opportunity**: NSW Department of Communities and Justice - Salesforce Application Managed Services  
**Analysis Date**: 4 December 2024  
**Competitor**: Deloitte Australia  
**Analysis Context**: Government human services, $8.5M-$16M annually, 15,000+ users

## Opportunity Summary

**Industry**: Government human services (child protection, social housing, disability, youth justice)  
**Service Type**: Salesforce Application Managed Services (Service Cloud, Experience Cloud, MuleSoft)  
**Budget**: $8.5M-$16M annually, total contract value up to $101.3M over 7 years  
**Client Type**: NSW Department of Communities and Justice - largest human services agency in Australia  
**Region**: New South Wales, Australia (metropolitan, regional, remote locations)  
**Scale**: 15,000+ staff, 500,000+ clients, 334 offices across NSW

## Key Strategic Insights

### Deloitte's Likely Approach
1. **Government Credibility Play**: Leverage strong federal relationships and international government success
2. **End-to-End Transformation**: Position as strategic transformation partner, not just technical vendor
3. **Risk and Compliance Leadership**: Emphasize security, compliance, and change management capabilities
4. **Global Best Practice**: Highlight international human services transformation methodologies

### Accenture's Counter-Strategy Focus Areas
1. **Australian Delivery Excellence**: Emphasize local delivery, NSW relationships, and Australian Salesforce ecosystem
2. **Technical Platform Mastery**: Leverage superior Salesforce certifications and platform-specific expertise
3. **Operational Excellence**: Highlight proven 24/7 support capabilities and AMS delivery track record
4. **Value-Based Positioning**: Compete on total value delivered rather than just strategic consulting reputation

### Critical Competitive Factors
- **Local delivery capability** (85% requirement favors Australian-focused providers)
- **Salesforce platform depth** (specialized certifications and experience matter)
- **24/7 operational support** (consulting model vs. operational excellence)
- **NSW government relationships** and understanding of DCJ's specific context
- **Cost competitiveness** while maintaining quality standards
