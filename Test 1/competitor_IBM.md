## Strategic Insights

### IBM's Likely Positioning
IBM will position themselves as the "safe" choice for government, leveraging their security credentials, global scale, and existing NSW government relationships. They'll emphasize technical sophistication and enterprise-grade solutions.

### Key Vulnerabilities to Exploit
1. **Cost consciousness:** With only 15% weighting on price but mention of "budget constraints," position IBM's premium as poor value
2. **User adoption history:** IBM's technical focus contradicts DCJ's emphasis on user experience and adoption
3. **Local presence:** The 80% local team mandate plays against IBM's global delivery model
4. **Agility concerns:** DCJ's aggressive timeline (pilot in 6 months) doesn't suit IBM's enterprise methodology

### Recommended Accenture Win Strategy
1. **Humanize the solution:** While IBM talks technology, focus on the 15,000 users struggling with 15+ clicks and printed forms
2. **Leverage relationships:** Use executive connections to understand IBM's proposal themes and counter accordingly
3. **Emphasize practical outcomes:** IBM will promise transformation - focus on specific pain points like system crashes during child protection crises
4. **Challenge qualifications:** Request clarification on whether IBM's Premier Partner status and case studies are from ANZ entity specifically
