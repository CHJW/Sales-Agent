## 2. MANDATORY/PASS-FAIL REQUIREMENTS

### Mandatory Requirements
*Source: Section 4.3*

**PASS/FAIL - Must meet ALL to proceed:**
- Current Salesforce Premier Partner status
- Minimum 5 government Salesforce implementations >$2M
- Security clearance capability (minimum NV1 for key personnel)
- Local Australian delivery team (minimum 80%)
- ISO 27001 certification
- Professional Indemnity Insurance ($20M minimum)

### Performance Guarantees
*Source: Section 5.2*
- User adoption: minimum 85% active usage within 6 months of go-live
- System performance: <2 second response times for 95% of transactions
- Availability: 99.9% uptime during business hours (6am-8pm AEST)
- Penalties: 2% of monthly fees per percentage point below targets

## STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

### Weight Distribution Analysis
- **Technical Focus (60%):** Combined Technical Solution (35%) + Implementation Approach (25%) represents 60% of total score
- **Price Secondary (15%):** Relatively low weighting suggests quality over cost approach
- **Experience Important (20%):** Significant emphasis on proven capability
- **Support Minimal (5%):** Low priority on ongoing support model

### Unusual Weightings or Criteria
- **High Technical Weighting:** 35% for technical solution is above typical government RFPs (usually 20-30%)
- **Low Price Weighting:** 15% is unusually low for government procurement (typically 25-40%)
- **Specific Experience Requirements:** Mandatory 5 government implementations >$2M is quite specific
- **Cultural Fit Inclusion:** "Local presence and cultural fit" (2 points) indicates relationship importance

### Client Priority Insights
- **Quality over Cost:** Low price weighting indicates DCJ prioritizes solution quality
- **Risk Mitigation:** High emphasis on proven experience and technical capability
- **User Adoption Critical:** Significant weight on change management and user experience
- **Security Paramount:** 7 points dedicated specifically to security/compliance approach
- **Integration Complexity:** 8 points for integration suggests this is a key challenge area

### Red Flags and Areas for Clarification
- **Presentation Weight:** Final scoring includes presentations but no specific weighting provided
- **Reference Check Impact:** Process mentions reference checks but no scoring mechanism defined
- **Government Experience Definition:** "Government sector experience" criteria lacks specific parameters
- **Local Team Definition:** "Minimum 80% local" needs clarification on geographic boundaries
- **Innovation Scoring:** "Innovation and value-add features" (5 points) subjective criteria may need clarification

### Competitive Positioning Recommendations
Given user context (competitors: TCS, IBM, Deloitte; win themes: existing DCJ relationship, leadership connections; weakness: ANZ Salesforce case studies):

1. **Leverage Existing Relationship:** Emphasize current SAP project success and leadership connections in Team Capability section
2. **Address Case Study Gap:** Focus on transferable government experience and methodology rather than regional Salesforce cases
3. **Differentiate on User Adoption:** Given 25% weight on implementation approach and change management, this is a key differentiator
4. **Technical Innovation:** 5 points for innovation provides opportunity to stand out from larger competitors
