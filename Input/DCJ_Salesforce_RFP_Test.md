### 1.2 Opportunity Details
**Project Name:** Community Services Digital Transformation - Salesforce Implementation  
**Industry:** Government - Human Services  
**Geographic Scope:** New South Wales, Australia (with national expansion potential)  
**Estimated Contract Value:** AUD $8.5M - $12M (implementation) + AUD $2.5M - $4M (optional support)  
**Contract Type:** Professional Services + Software Licensing + Support Services  

**Current Incumbent:** Legacy system managed by internal IT team (mainframe-based COBOL system from 1987)

### 1.3 Project Scope
The Department requires a **greenfield Salesforce implementation** to replace our legacy case management system serving 15,000+ community services workers across NSW. This is a **people-oriented department** focused on child protection, disability services, housing assistance, and community support programs.

**Critical Success Factors:**
- Must handle sensitive personal information with absolute security
- User adoption is paramount - current staff struggle with complex legacy systems
- Real-time collaboration across 200+ offices essential
- Mobile-first approach for field workers mandatory

## 2. REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Functional Requirements

#### R001 - Core Platform (MANDATORY)
- Salesforce Service Cloud implementation
- Case management for 50,000+ active cases
- 360-degree client view across all DCJ services
- Advanced workflow automation for compliance processes
- Integration with existing NSW Government systems

#### R002 - User Experience (MANDATORY)
- Intuitive interface requiring minimal training
- Mobile application for field workers (iOS/Android)
- Offline capability for remote areas with poor connectivity
- Accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA standard)
- Multi-language support (English, Arabic, Mandarin, Vietnamese)

#### R003 - Data Security & Compliance (MANDATORY)
- PROTECTED security classification handling
- Privacy Act 1988 compliance
- NSW Government ICT Policy compliance
- Audit trail for all data access and modifications
- Role-based access control with segregation of duties

#### R004 - Integration Requirements (MANDATORY)
- Real-time integration with Justice Health NSW
- Batch integration with Centrelink systems
- API integration with NSW Land Registry
- Single Sign-On (SSO) with Microsoft Active Directory
- Data synchronisation with existing financial systems (SAP)

#### R005 - Reporting & Analytics (MANDATORY)
- Executive dashboard with KPIs
- Compliance reporting (automated generation)
- Workload management reporting
- Client outcome tracking and analytics
- Real-time operational reporting

#### R006 - Training & Change Management (HIGHLY DESIRABLE)
- Comprehensive training program for 15,000 users
- Change management support
- User adoption strategy and measurement
- Ongoing capability building program

#### R007 - Business Continuity (DESIRABLE)
- Disaster recovery solution (RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 1 hour)
- High availability (99.9% uptime during business hours)
- Backup and restoration procedures
- Business continuity planning support

### 2.2 Technical Specifications

**Platform Requirements:**
- Salesforce Enterprise Edition minimum
- Must support 15,000 concurrent users
- 50GB+ data storage capacity
- API rate limits: minimum 100,000 calls per day
- Response time: <2 seconds for standard operations

**Infrastructure Requirements:**
- Cloud-based solution preferred (AWS Sydney region)
- Data sovereignty: all data must remain in Australia
- Encryption in transit and at rest (AES-256)
- Network security: dedicated VPN connection
- Monitoring and alerting capabilities

## 8. ADDITIONAL INFORMATION

### 8.1 Current State Challenges

**Legacy System Pain Points:**
- System crashes frequently during peak periods (child protection crisis periods)
- Takes 15+ clicks to complete basic case updates
- No mobile access - workers carry printed forms
- Reporting takes days to generate - management flies blind
- Data silos prevent holistic client view
- Training new staff takes 6 months due to system complexity
- Manual processes for inter-agency communication
- No real-time collaboration between case workers

**Organisational Pain Points:**
- High staff turnover (25% annually) partly due to system frustration
- Compliance reporting is manual and error-prone
- Client complaints about inconsistent service due to information gaps
- Management lacks visibility into workloads and outcomes
- Audit findings consistently highlight data quality issues

### 8.2 Success Criteria
- **User Adoption:** 90% of staff actively using system within 12 months
- **Efficiency Gains:** 30% reduction in administrative time
- **Data Quality:** 95% accuracy in key data fields
- **Client Satisfaction:** 15% improvement in client feedback scores
- **Compliance:** Zero audit findings related to data management
- **Staff Satisfaction:** 20% improvement in technology-related survey scores

### 8.3 Stakeholder Context
- **Executive Team:** Focused on compliance, risk reduction, and demonstrable value
- **Frontline Workers:** Need simple, fast, mobile-friendly tools
- **Middle Management:** Require real-time visibility and reporting
- **IT Department:** Concerned about security, integration complexity, and ongoing support burden
- **Clients:** Expect consistent, timely service regardless of which office they visit

### 8.4 Known Challenges and Risks
- **Change Resistance:** Historical resistance to technology changes
- **Data Migration:** Complex data structures in legacy system
- **Integration Complexity:** 25+ separate systems require various integration approaches
- **Geographic Spread:** Remote offices with limited connectivity
- **Regulatory Environment:** Frequent changes in government policies and requirements
- **Budget Constraints:** Fixed budget envelope with limited contingency

### 8.5 Competitive Intelligence Hints
- **Previous consultations with:** Deloitte (enterprise architecture review), PwC (business case development), KPMG (change management strategy)
- **Technology preferences indicated:** "Cloud-first strategy," "Platform approach preferred over custom development"
- **Incumbent relationships:** Current maintenance contract with DXC Technology expires June 2025
- **Procurement history:** Preference for fixed-price contracts with clear milestone-based payments

### 8.6 Inconsistencies and Ambiguities 
*(These are intentionally included to test the Inconsistency prompt)*

- **Timeline Conflict:** Section 3.1 states 36-month implementation, but Section 7.1 shows contract award February 2025 with March start, suggesting completion by March 2028. However, Section 6.1 mentions 3-year head contract implying February 2028 completion.

- **User Count Discrepancy:** Section 1.3 mentions 15,000+ community services workers, Section 2.2 specifies 15,000 concurrent users, but Section 3.1 Phase 2 mentions "5,000 users across metro Sydney" suggesting not all workers are in Sydney.

- **Budget Range Overlap:** Opportunity overview estimates $8.5M-$12M implementation + $2.5M-$4M support, but Section 6.1 mentions "up to $16M over 6 years" which doesn't align with the upper ranges ($12M + $4M = $16M exactly).

- **Response Time Requirements:** Section 2.2 specifies "<2 seconds for standard operations" but Section 6.3 mentions "<2 second response times for 95% of transactions" - unclear if these are the same requirement.

- **Support Service Definition:** Section 3.2 describes support services as "optional" but Section 5.2 requires pricing for "optional support services" as part of the mandatory response.
